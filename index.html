Who F3 is built for

You do not have to be the best player in the group.

You do have to be willing to work.

F3 is built for female flag football players—and it's not reserved for those with the biggest names, highest rankings, longest highlight reels, or most social media attention. It's for athletes who want to improve their movement, technique, football understanding, confidence, preparation, and ability to execute under pressure.

We are selecting athletes based on commitment, coachability, consistency, availability, and fit—not reputation.

The F3 coaching team

High-level experience. Personal development.

F3 brings together accomplished athletes and football leaders who understand that sustainable improvement requires more than athletic ability. Each coach brings a different perspective. Every athlete is held to the same standard of preparation, coachability, effort, and attention to detail.

Morris Claiborne

Morris Claiborne

NFL First-Round Pick · Jim Thorpe Award Winner · LSU All-American

Morris Claiborne developed from a high school athlete in Shreveport, Louisiana, into one of the most accomplished defensive backs in college football.

At LSU, he earned consensus All-American honors and won the Jim Thorpe Award as the nation's top defensive back. He was selected sixth overall in the NFL Draft and went on to play nine professional seasons.

Morris brings athletes a firsthand understanding of defensive technique, preparation, competitive discipline, film study, movement, and the details required to perform at the highest levels.

Kevin Dickens Jr.

Kevin Dickens Jr.

Five-Time Flag Football All-American · National Champion · Athlete Developer

Kevin Dickens Jr. brings more than two decades of competitive flag football experience and a deep understanding of the modern flag game.

A former collegiate track and field athlete at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, Kevin became a five-time Flag Football All-American and a collegiate flag football national champion.

Beyond his playing accomplishments, Kevin has built and led competitive flag football programs, coached athletes, organized high-level competition, and helped create development pathways for youth, women's, and adult players.

He brings F3 athletes a combination of flag-specific intelligence, movement expertise, competitive experience, and an ability to translate the game into practical instruction.

Stephanie Raymond

Stephanie Raymond

WNBA Draft Pick · Professional Athlete · Championship Quarterback

Stephanie Raymond brings an exceptional multi-sport background and a powerful perspective on leadership, decision-making, and athlete development.

After a standout career at Northern Illinois University, Stephanie was selected by the Chicago Sky in the 2007 WNBA Draft. She continued her basketball career professionally, including several seasons competing internationally.

Stephanie later transitioned into football, competing in flag football before becoming the quarterback of the Chicago Blitz. She helped lead the team to a championship and earned Offensive Player of the Year recognition.

Her experience developing across multiple sports gives her a unique understanding of vision, anticipation, movement, communication, leadership, and the process of learning and performing a position at a high level.

  • ✓Care about becoming technically better

  • ✓Are willing to be challenged

How selection works

Earn your place in the founding class.

The application process protects the quality of the training environment. Small groups only work when the athletes are willing to participate fully, accept feedback, attend consistently, and contribute to the standard of the group.

1

Apply

A parent or guardian completes the athlete application.

2

Review

The F3 team reviews age, experience, goals, position, availability, commitment, and developmental fit.

3

Placement

Accepted athletes are placed into the cohort that best fits their age, physical maturity, experience, position, and current developmental needs.

4

Enroll

The parent completes payment, registration, waiver, and onboarding to secure the athlete's place.

Submitting an application does not guarantee acceptance. Cohorts will be kept intentionally small, and athletes must be a strong fit for the program standards and available group.
